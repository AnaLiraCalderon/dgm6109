Data Visualization Analysis: The Emojis Stress Calendar

HYPOTHESIS:"As my daily time playing The Sims 4 increases my stress decreases. One other factor that could impact my stress is the temperature of the day."

Can external factors such as playing The Sims 4 and daily temperature effectively reduce my stress levels?

Based on the visualization, the data reveals an interesting pattern: playing The Sims 4 is most effective at reducing stress on Cold Days , where even moderate play sessions tend to result in a relaxed or tense emoji outcome. On Hot Dayshowever, stress levels remain higher regardless of playtime, suggesting that high temperatures may counteract the relaxing benefits of the game.

EXPLANATION: The visualization is structured as a calendar grid, where each entry represents one day of observation. At the center of each daily record, a colored circle indicates the temperature of that day, as shown in the Temperature legend: blue for Cold Days (<=5°C), orange for Moderate days (>5°C and <=10°C), and red for Hot Days (>10°C). Inside each circle, a specific emoji represents the resulting stress level after playing The Sims 4, as shown in the Stress Level After legend: 😌 for Relaxed (0-1), 😐 for Tense (2-3), and 😵 for Very Stressed (4-5).

Two additional data layers provide further context. At the top of each entry, the playtime duration in minutes and the date shows how long The Sims 4 was played that day. At the bottom, the Time of Day legend categorizes each session as Morning (10:00 a.m. - 12:00 p.m.), Afternoon (12:00 p.m. - 5:00 p.m.), Evening (5:00 p.m. - 8:00 p.m.), or None for days when The Sims 4 was not played.

CONCLUSION: In conclusion, the data supports the hypothesis. The visualization shows that the most effective stress reduction occurs when The Sims 4 is played for longer sessions (40-60 minutes) on Cold Days (<=5°C) during the Evening. For example, on January 30, playing 60 minutes in the Evening at 9°C reduced stress from 3 to 1 (😐 to 😌), and on February 10, a 32-minute Evening session at 3°C dropped stress from 1 to 0 (😌). This combination consistently produced the most relaxed emoji outcomes (😌 0-1), confirming that both playtime duration and temperature are key factors in stress regulation.

On the other hand, short sessions (0-30 minutes) on Hot Days (>10°C) showed the highest stress levels (😵 4-5), suggesting that high temperatures reduce the effectiveness of The Sims 4 as a stress relief tool. One notable exception is February 4, where stress was 0 (😌) despite no playtime. However, this was the hottest day in the dataset at 16°C, suggesting that on naturally low-stress days, temperature alone may have been the dominant factor. Moderate temperature days (>5°C and <=10°C) showed mixed results, where playtime duration became the deciding factor between a relaxed or tense outcome. Overall, the data confirms that increasing daily playtime is the strongest predictor of stress reduction, with temperature acting as a secondary but significant influence.
