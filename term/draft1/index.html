Data Visualization Analysis: The Emojis Stress Calendar

Hypothesis:As daily temperature increases, my stress reduction levels decrease.

While the core hypothesis explores how daily temperature impacts stress reduction, the visualization provides a deeper narrative by incorporating playtime duration and session timing as additional data layers.

Explanation:Beyond temperature (represented by the colored borders), I have included the playtime duration in minutes at the top of each entry. This third factor allows us to see if a longer session of The Sims 4 can overcome the negative impact of a Hot Day. Furthermore, each entry is categorized by Time of Day (Morning, Afternoon, or Evening).At the center of each daily record, I have used specific emojis to represent the resulting stress level after play the sims 4. These icons act as the primary visual outcome, ranging from a "Relaxed" state (0-1), "tense (2-1)" and "Very Stressed" state (4-5).

Conclusion:In conclusion, the data partially supports the hypothesis. While temperature does play a role in overall comfort, playtime duration and session timing are the dominant factors in emotional regulation. My findings suggest that for The Sims 4 to effectively reduce stress, a minimum threshold of 40 minutes is required. Furthermore, the "Evening" context acts as a multiplier for relaxation, likely because it signals the end of daily responsibilities, allowing the game's mechanics to more effectively lower stress regardless of the external temperature.
